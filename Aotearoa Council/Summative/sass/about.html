Mohiohio / About Us

ACW has a long and proud history promoting improvements to the quality of life of women, families and the community.

Our actions have helped shape the social and economic fabric of our country. Today our work focuses on realising our vision, a gender equal New Zealand.

Kaupapa - Who are we

Aotearoa's Council of Wāhine is an umbrella organisation working towards true gender equality in Aotearoa New Zealand. Established in 1896, we have 13 branches and more than 200 organisations, as well as individuals, as members.

Over the past 126 years we have led and supported many initiatives that have benefited all tāngata Aotearoa/people of New Zealand. We are committing to working in a collaborative, inclusive way – honouring Te Tiriti o Waitangi, being guided by feminism and constantly learning.

Learn More

The ACW's strategic vision is a Gender Equal New Zealand. By building understanding and driving action for gender equality, we enable tāngata Aotearoa/people of New Zealand to have the freedom and opportunity to determine their own future.

We want all tāngata Aotearoa to say, “gender is irrelevant in terms of achievement and freedom”; “all societal roles are valued in New Zealand”; “I can be whatever I want to be” and “employment supports gender equality and work/life balance”.

At the moment not all tāngata Aotearoa can say these things. Our research shows that if we are to be successful in achieving our vision of a gender equal New Zealand the potential impact is huge – socially, and economically.

Our Mission

Our mission is to achieve gender equality in Aotearoa through five key areas:

Economic independence; Safety and Health; Education; Influence and Decision Making; and Environmental Sustainability.

Leadership

ACW’s board members are drawn from a diverse constituency of policy makers, academics, researchers, activists, funders, practitioners, and business people committed to gender equality in Aotearoa.
